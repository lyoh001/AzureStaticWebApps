PROBLEM STATEMENT

Information obtained from the Ethiopian Coffee and Tea Authority revealed that coffee berry disease (CBD), CWD, and coffee leaf rust (CLR) are the three major fungal diseases of Arabica coffee, which are causing a significant reduction in coffee production and consumption in the country. These diseases can cause severe damage to the coffee plants, leading to reduced yields and a decline in the quality of the coffee beans. The traditional approach employed for illness surveillance is observation with the naked eye, which is time-consuming, expensive, and requires significant competence. This approach is also prone to human error, leading to inaccurate identification and treatment of the diseases. Therefore, it is important to find an alternative solution that can automatically identify the diseases without the need for experts. One potential solution is the use of deep learning, object detection, and image classification to solve this problem. These technologies have been widely used in various fields such as medical imaging, self-driving cars, and facial recognition, and have shown great potential in the identification and management of plant diseases. Deep learning methods have been introduced for the detection of different types of coffee plant diseases caused by pests and pathogens. These diseases can be classified by machine learning techniques like segmentation, and classification along with the estimation of the severity of stress. By using deep learning algorithms, the system can identify patterns and features in the images of the infected plants that are not visible to the human eye. Additionally, object detection can be used to identify specific regions of the coffee plant that are affected by the disease, allowing for targeted treatment. This can help farmers to apply the right type and amount of treatment to the affected areas, reducing the use of chemicals and increasing the efficiency of the treatment. Image classification can also be used to accurately identify the type of disease present on the plant, such as CBD, CWD, or CLR. This can help farmers to choose the most appropriate treatment for the specific disease, increasing the chances of successful treatment. Furthermore, using deep learning models can also help in predicting the potential spread of the disease, helping farmers to take preventative measures and avoiding widespread crop damage. This can include early detection and treatment of the disease, as well as identifying and isolating infected plants to prevent the spread of the disease to healthy plants. The use of such technology can significantly reduce the time and cost associated with manual disease identification, as well as increasing the accuracy and efficiency of disease identification and management. By leveraging the power of deep learning, object detection, and image classification, farmers can better protect their crops and increase their yields, leading to a more sustainable and profitable coffee industry in Ethiopia.
