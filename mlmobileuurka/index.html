PROBLEM STATEMENT

Approximately 70% of all maternal mortality cases in the world occur in Sub-Saharan Africa countries because of unequally despaired, under-resourced healthcare settings and low numbers of skilled healthcare workers. Somalia has the 6th highest MMR worldwide, with 1 in 18 dying from pregnancy-related complications. Unfortunately, most of these complications could have been easily cured, avoided, or managed. The technology nowadays allows for remote tracking of women's health status, which is vital since 70% of the population lives outside urban areas with low or no access to healthcare facilities. The urgency for the technology is high because the average age in Somalia is 17 years, and the typical childbearing age is between 15 and 45, implying that there will be an exponential increase in pregnancies over the next three decades. In the next two years, the need will increase by 25%, but the shortage of healthcare workers remains.

PROJECT GOALS

The AI solution that will be built will let a person send her matrix of data (related to personal health) and the AI solution will predict the potential health hazards the person may encounter during pregnancy. The goal of such an AI system will be to reduce maternal mortality. Optionally, the AI challenge may also build an end-to-end tool that will let a person upload her data via the tool and the person can see the outcome from the AI model.
